## Core mechanisms

**Five advantages of international trade:** increased revenues, decreased competition, faster growth, risk diversification, surplus disposal.

**Four challenges:** business landscape understanding (ethnocentrism risk), business politics, regulations, cultural differences.

**Six entry modes (least to most involved):** exporting → franchising → licensing → joint venture → strategic alliance → foreign direct investment.

**Standardized vs. adapted marketing strategy.** Coca-Cola exemplifies standardized; Netflix exemplifies adapted.

## Vocabularies

**Seven readiness characteristics (Quackenbos et al., HBR):**
- Attitude (priority)
- Aptitude (skills)
- Magnitude (scope alignment)
- Latitude (adaptability)
- Rectitude (legal/ethical flexibility)
- Exactitude (corporate culture)
- Fortitude (long-term commitment)

**Three income types:**
- Consumer income (household earnings)
- Disposable income (after taxes)
- Discretionary income (after taxes + essentials)

**Trade regulations:** tariffs, quotas, trade blocs, embargoes.

**Three product strategies:** straight extension, product adaptation, product invention.

**Three firm classifications:** international (any global), multinational (centralized — Amazon), transnational (high localization — McDonald's).

**Hofstede's five cultural dimensions:**
- Power Distance Index (PDI) — US 40, world avg 55
- Individualism vs. Collectivism (IDV)
- Masculinity vs. Femininity (MAS)
- Uncertainty Avoidance (UAI)
- Long-term vs. Short-term Orientation (LTO)

## Named trade-offs

- **Same-product economics vs. localized-product relevance.** Standardized = cheap + risky; adapted = expensive + durable.
- **Assessment thoroughness vs. time to market.** Layered approach: fast major-risks first, slower deep assessment after committing.
- **Entry mode involvement vs. risk exposure.** Lower-involvement modes have less revenue capture; higher-involvement modes have more capital exposure.

## Ethics flags

- **Ethnocentrism** — assuming the new market works like home market.
- **Dumping** (illegal, distinct from surplus disposal).
- **Exploitation** of weak labor protections / environmental regulations.
- **"Made in" / country-of-origin labeling** — false claims violate consumer protection law.
- **Cultural insensitivity** in marketing material (Nivea, Gucci).
