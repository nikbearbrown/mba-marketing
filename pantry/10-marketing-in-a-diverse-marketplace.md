## Core mechanisms

**Diversity marketing as adaptation strategy** — adapting the marketing mix to specific cultural and sociodemographic segments within a single market. Subset of broader diversity marketing.

**Hall's iceberg model** — culture is 10% tangible/external (visible) + 90% intangible/internal (subconscious). Marketing failures most often happen below the waterline.

**Standardization vs. adaptation** — applies domestically as well as internationally (Apple = standardized; Ford D4 platform / Zoom pandemic adaptation = adapted).

## Named trade-offs

- **Adaptation cost vs. inclusion benefit.** Each adapted campaign multiplies cost but inclusion data (61% Adobe, 67% Top Design, 83% Heat) suggests inclusion side has been underestimated.
- **Segmentation precision vs. stereotype risk.** Same tools produce both depending on use; research-grounded vs. assumption-grounded distinguishes.

## Recurring named cases

- **P&G** — 1961 Spanish-language ad on Univisión; 1965 EEOC affirmative action; 2014 "Like a Girl" stereotype-challenging campaign; 2021 "Widen the Screen" + Marc Pritchard supply-chain inclusion principle; Black creators 6% of films made.
- **Tarte Cosmetics Shape Tape Foundation 2018** — 15 shades mostly light; backlash; revised.
- **Burger King "Texican Whopper"** — Mexican wrestler stereotype + flag-as-costume; revised after backlash.
- **Target "Sobremesa" / #SinTraducción** — Hispanic millennial campaign; cultural concept without English equivalent.
- **9Lives cat food** — works in English (cats have 9 lives); Spanish/Portuguese idiom = 7 lives.
- **Rainbow flag** — LGBTQIA+ in US; resembles Andean rainbow flag (Inca empire) with 2 blue stripes.
- **IKEA #ProgressIsMade Pride 2021** — $50K to GLSEN; Canada Pride loveseats.
- **Apple** — standardization exemplar (same products globally with power-source variations).
- **Ford D4 platform** — adaptation across Ford and Lincoln models.
- **Zoom 2019–2020** — 10M → 300M users in 5 months.
- **Nike "Nike by You"** — customer-customization adaptation.
- **Sherman Antitrust Act** — fair-competition foundation.
