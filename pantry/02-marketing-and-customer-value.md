## Core mechanism

**The value ratio** $V = B/P$ тАФ customer value as the ratio of perceived benefits to price. Treat as the chapter's central equation; refer back wherever pricing, positioning, value proposition, or competitor comparison is the question. Customer's *B* is squishy, layered, and partly unconscious тАФ the firm sets inputs but the customer sets the ratio.

## Vocabularies (reusable across the rest of the book)

**Four value types** тАФ the structure underneath *B*:
- Functional (what the product does)
- Monetary (what the price feels like relative to perceived worth)
- Social (how the product connects the customer to others)
- Psychological (how the product makes the customer feel about themselves)

**Five layers of need** тАФ what to listen for when a customer says what they want:
- Stated (said out loud)
- Real (what the stated need actually means)
- Unstated (expected but not asked)
- Delight (a positive surprise)
- Secret (admitted to no one, sometimes not even themselves)

**Four utilities** тАФ kinds of usefulness inside *B*:
- Form (finished thing тАФ the donut, not the flour)
- Time (available when you want it тАФ 24/7 stores)
- Place (available where you want it тАФ Uber Eats)
- Possession (easy to own тАФ auto loans, BNPL)

**The 4Ps (marketing mix)** тАФ the firm's four levers:
- Product (what is offered)
- Price (what the customer pays)
- Place (where the customer can access тАФ *not* the firm's location)
- Promotion (how the customer learns and is persuaded)
- Internal-consistency rule: pull one P, the others must follow.

**Three nested environments**:
- Internal тАФ 5 Ms (Minds, Minutes, Machinery, Materials, Money) + organizational culture
- Microenvironment тАФ Suppliers, Market Intermediaries, Customers, Competitors, General Public
- Macroenvironment тАФ PESTLE (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental)
- Trade-off rule: spend most decision-making energy on what you control, most attention on what you cannot.

## Frames

**Five-step marketing process** тАФ the operational loop:
1. Understand the marketplace and customers
2. Develop a customer-driven marketing strategy (target market + value proposition)
3. Deliver high customer value (run the mix)
4. Grow profitable customer relationships (CRM)
5. Capture customer value as profit (customer equity)

**Five evolutionary marketing concepts** тАФ historical specifications of "what is the customer for the firm":
- Production concept (postтАУCivil War to 1920s) тАФ availability and price
- Product concept (1920sтАУ1950s) тАФ quality and features; *marketing myopia* failure mode
- Sales concept (1950s) тАФ push and persuade
- Marketing concept тАФ start with customer needs, build to fit
- Societal marketing concept тАФ balance customer wants, firm capability, society's long-term interest

**Customer / consumer / buyer distinction** тАФ the grandmother (customer) buying a toy for the grandson (consumer); the buyer initiates the exchange.

**Exchange process** тАФ buyer with want or need + seller with desired object + value transfer, where value is the perceived benefit relative to cost.

**Internal vs. external interested parties** тАФ interested parties = stakeholders; internal = employees, owners, managers, investors; external = customers, creditors, suppliers, distributors, society at large.

**Internal marketing** тАФ marketing directed at employees as the deliverers of customer experience.

**CRM** тАФ the systems and discipline of tracking, managing, and analyzing customer interactions; three flavors (operational, analytical, collaborative).

**Customer loyalty / customer retention / customer equity** тАФ the long-game concepts that turn one sale into many.

**Value proposition** тАФ the compressed, single-sentence promise the firm makes to the target customer.

## Named trade-offs (the MKBHD move тАФ reusable as a structural unit)

The chapter's three concept sections each carry a named trade-off. These compress the central tension of each framework into one line and are quotable across the rest of the book.

- **$V = B/P$ тАФ raise $B$ vs. cut $P$.** *Cutting P is fast, visible, copyable. Raising B is slow, expensive, and hard to copy. The firms that sustain dominant positions almost always raise the numerator instead of dropping the denominator.*
- **The 4Ps тАФ coupling.** *All four levers must lean the same direction. Pulling one without pulling the others tells the customer two stories at once. Confusion is death.*
- **Three environments тАФ control vs. attention.** *Spend most of your decision-making energy on what you control, but most of your attention on what you cannot.*

A chapter that doesn't name its trade-off has not yet earned the voice. Use these as templates: each subsequent chapter should produce 2тАУ3 named trade-offs of the same shape.
