## Core moves

**The "marketing is not a department" reframing.** Marketing is the work of understanding what customers actually value and using that understanding across product, price, channel, and message decisions — regardless of which department makes them. Engineers, finance, operations, and product all make marketing decisions when their decisions touch a customer.

**The three-unit architecture of the book.** Unit 1 (Foundations: Chapters 1–3), Unit 2 (Audience: Chapters 4–10), Unit 3 (Levers: Chapters 11–22). Each unit answers a different question — *what is value*, *who values it*, *how to deliver it*. Reuse this scaffolding when chapters reference unit boundaries.

**The "what this book is not" frame.** Sets expectations by negation: not a course in advertising; not a course in selling; not a how-to manual for any specific platform; not skeptical-of-marketing-by-default. Useful when the book has to explain why it spends time on a topic the reader didn't expect — or when it ignores a topic the reader expected.
