## Core mechanisms

**STP as a sequence.** Segment → Target → Position. Each step constrains the next. A firm that segments demographically can't suddenly target on values without redoing the segmentation. The three steps make sense only as a sequence.

**ADAMS criteria** for evaluating whether a segment is worth pursuing:
- **A**ccessible — can you reach it at affordable cost?
- **D**ifferentiable — internally homogeneous, externally heterogeneous?
- **A**ctionable — can you execute strategy that produces measurable outcomes?
- **M**easurable — can you estimate the segment size?
- **S**ubstantial — is it large enough to justify dedicated investment?

**Positioning** (per Ries & Trout): *"manipulate what's already up there in the mind"* — not creating something new but shaping how customers perceive what already exists.

## Vocabularies

**Four consumer segmentation methods (the where / who / how / why):**
- Geographic — location, climate, language, density
- Demographic — gender, income, age, education, race, religion, ethnicity, occupation, family structure
- Behavioral — benefit (Crest variants), occasion (Valentine's chocolates), usage (Pareto 80/20)
- Psychographic — AIO (activities/interests/opinions); VALS (8 types: Innovators, Thinkers, Believers, Achievers, Strivers, Experiencers, Makers, Survivors); values-based

**B2B segmentation methods:**
- Firmographic (industry, location, size, legal structure, performance)
- Technographic (hardware/software stack)
- Needs-based (Levitt: "want a hole, not a drill")
- Value-based / tiering / profitability segmentation
- Behavioral

**International segmentation methods:**
- Geographic (regions + infrastructure constraints)
- Political/legal (government, tariffs, sanctions)
- Economic (developed/developing/underdeveloped per UN)
- Cultural (Hofstede dimensions: PDI, individualism/collectivism, masculinity/femininity, uncertainty avoidance, long/short orientation)

**Four target-market strategies:**
- Undifferentiated (mass — Coca-Cola)
- Differentiated (segmented — Marriott portfolio)
- Concentrated (niche — LUSH, In-N-Out)
- Micromarketing (customized — Stitch Fix, real estate)

**Two positioning approaches:**
- Head-to-head (Avis vs. Hertz; Wendy's vs. McDonald's)
- Differentiation (Curves; LEGO STEAM)

**Target market vs. target audience:** market = whose needs are designed for; audience = whose attention is captured. May overlap or not (LEGO: market = children, audience = parents).

**Buyer personas:** semi-fictional representations from real data; typically 3–8 per firm; named (Best Buy: Buzz, Barry, Ray, Jill).

**Positioning statement templates (two standard):**
- *"[Brand] provides [benefit] for [customers] who [need] because [reason]."*
- *"For [audience], [brand] is the [market] that delivers [differentiation] so they can [benefit] because [evidence]."*

**Perceptual map:** 2D diagram with two determinant attributes; firm + 5–10 competitors plotted; reads for own position, competitor clusters, white-space gaps.

## Named trade-offs (the MKBHD move)

- **Precision vs. cost.** Finer segmentation = more relevant marketing but more expensive to execute. ADAMS resolves.
- **Scale economics vs. relevance economics.** Mass marketing scales; micromarketing converts. Choose based on customer heterogeneity + firm resources.
- **Head-to-head visibility vs. differentiation defensibility.** Head-to-head wins by direct comparison but reinforces leader; differentiation creates new demand but takes longer.

## Ethics flags

- Children/teens — APA: under 7–8 can't discern persuasive intent; gender stereotyping (Disney Princess vs. Transformers); childhood obesity link to children's TV ads; Brach's, Lemonhead, Ghirardelli, Jelly Belly, PEEPS, Mike and Ike, Welch's voluntarily avoid under-12 advertising; Juul case.
- Elderly — fixed income + health vulnerabilities; misleading prescription claims; COVID "cure-alls."
- Low-income — high-APR cards, MLM schemes, exclusion from opportunity.
- Ethnic/racial profiling — Facebook HUD lawsuit (algorithm allowed exclusion by race/gender/religion in jobs/housing/credit ads).
- Subway × Let's Move! ($41M / 3 years; "Playtime: Powered by Veggies"; PHA partnership) — positive case.
