## Core mechanisms

- **Product-service continuum** — most offerings are bundles of tangible product + intangible service + experience.
- **Customer experience (CX)** — the impression formed across every touchpoint; integrates technology, marketing, sales, service.
- **Product hierarchy:** product item → product line → product mix; line depth = items per line; mix width = number of lines.
- **Product life cycle:** introduction → growth → maturity → decline. Not all products move linearly.
- **Brand equity** = price premium over substitute. **Brand value** = financial asset.
- **Keller's brand equity model:** identity → meaning → feelings/judgments → resonance.

## Vocabularies

**Consumer product classification:** convenience / shopping / specialty / unsought.
**Industrial product classification:** materials and parts / capital items / supplies and services.
**Line stretching:** downward (Tesla S → 3) / upward / both ways.
**Line filling** vs. stretching.
**Introduction-stage pricing:** rapid skimming / slow skimming (Tesla initial) / rapid penetration (Old Navy 1994 $8 jeans) / slow penetration.
**Brand development:** line extension (Oreo flavors) / brand extension (Starbucks bottled coffees) / multibrands (Nabisco's Chips Ahoy + Oreo + Teddy Grahams) / new brands (Starbucks owns Ethos Water).
**Loyalty types:** switchers / shifting loyals (Toyota↔Honda) / split loyals (Gap+BR+J.Crew) / hard-core loyals.
**Packaging functions:** protection / brand expression / convenience and safety / usability / compliance / differentiation.

## Named trade-offs

- **Bundling vs. unbundling** — bundle for higher per-customer revenue + switching costs; unbundle for lower friction + price competition.
- **Portfolio breadth vs. coherence** — wide mix captures more occasions; narrow mix simplifies and avoids cannibalization.
