## Core mechanisms

**The buyer's black box (stimulus-response model).** Inputs (4Ps + environmental stimuli) → buyer characteristics + buyer decision process (the box) → buying response (what / when / where / how often / how much). The interior is partially inferable through frameworks, partially mysterious. Marketing research is the discipline of inferring more.

**Five-stage consumer decision process.** Need recognition → information search → evaluation of alternatives → purchase decision → post-purchase evaluation. Stages can be skipped for low-involvement purchases; all five are present for high-involvement purchases.

**Maslow's hierarchy of needs.** Five levels: physiological, safety/security, belonging/social, esteem, self-actualization. Lower levels generally take priority when threatened; marketing campaigns target specific levels. Lower-level appeals are universal-but-competitive; higher-level appeals are specialized-but-defensible.

## Vocabularies

**Five categories of influence on consumer behavior:**
- Cultural — culture, subculture, social class, gender
- Social — family (orientation + procreation), reference groups, roles & status
- Personal — age & life cycle, occupation, economic situation, lifestyle, personality & self-concept
- Psychological — motivation, perception, learning, beliefs & attitudes
- Situational — environmental atmospherics, social situation, goal of trip, timing, mood

**Four types of buying behavior** (involvement × brand differentiation matrix):
- Complex (high / significant) — new car, major electronics
- Dissonance-reducing (high / few) — ceramic tile, mid-tier home renovation
- Habitual (low / few) — milk, paper towels
- Variety-seeking (low / significant) — tortilla chips, candy, craft beer

**Three perceptual processes:**
- Selective attention (filtering inputs by relevance)
- Selective distortion (interpreting to fit existing beliefs)
- Selective retention (remembering supportive evidence; forgetting contradictory)

**Heuristics:**
- Price = quality
- Brand loyalty
- Country of origin
- Most reviews / highest rating (modern variants)

**Generational cohorts (US):**
- Baby Boomers (1946–1964) — ~70M, $2.6T buying power
- Generation X (1965–1979/80) — ~65M
- Millennials / Gen Y (1981–1994/96) — ~72M (largest cohort)
- Generation Z / Zoomers (1997–2012) — 67+M

**Five social classes** (sociological standard): upper, upper-middle, lower-middle, working, economically disadvantaged.

## Named trade-offs (the MKBHD move)

- **Influence-mapping completeness vs. measurable specificity.** The five-category framework is comprehensive but unmanageable; working marketers learn which 1–2 categories dominate their category and instrument those.
- **Decision-process completeness vs. shortcutability.** All purchases potentially go through five stages; most everyday purchases skip Stages 2 and 3. Knowing which type of buying behavior the category triggers determines which stages need marketing investment.
- **Maslow lower vs. higher levels.** Lower-level appeals = bigger market, thinner differentiation. Higher-level appeals = smaller market, durable differentiation.

## Recurring named cases

- **McDonald's COVID 3Ds** — digital ($10B in 2020, ~20% system-wide), drive-through (30 sec saved → 300M extra customers), delivery (28k → 41k restaurants).
- **Run-DMC × Adidas (1986)** — first fashion-brand × musical-act endorsement deal; sneaker / hip-hop subculture mechanism. Subsequent: Master P × Converse, Jay-Z × Reebok / Puma, 50 Cent × Reebok, Missy Elliott × Adidas, Big Sean × Adidas, Drake × Nike (Air Jordan).
- **Pepsi Challenge** — blind taste test, Pepsi consistently won, Coca-Cola did not lose share (the Pepsi paradox); the canonical case for selective distortion.
- **Snickers** *"You're not you when you're hungry"* — Maslow physiological.
- **Allstate** *"You're in good hands"* — Maslow safety.
- **Lysol** *"What It Takes to Protect"* — Maslow safety.
- **Nike** *"Find Your Greatness"* — Maslow self-actualization.
- **Dove** self-esteem campaign — Maslow esteem.
- **Patagonia** — mission statement; fair-trade certified; supplier transparency to farm level; recycled materials, take-back program; ethical-consumerism canonical case.
- **Chipotle** — 130-mile sourcing radius; GMO-free positioning; lighter ethical-positioning case.
- **Abercrombie & Fitch** — "Fierce" cologne sprayed in stores; loud club music; situational-factors as marketing instrument.
- **Kendall Jenner** (217M+ Instagram followers); **Lionel Messi** (310M+) — modern reference-group / opinion-leader scale.
