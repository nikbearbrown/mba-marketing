### Open fact-check item

**Mintel "29 percent share via social media" — polarity check.** The chapter states: *"Twenty-nine percent share their negative experience via social media — which means the loss propagates."* Source m00013 reads: *"approximately one-third of consumers are inclined to tell others when they perceive a brand to be taking actions that they perceive to be honest, fair, and responsible. Taking this one step further, 29 percent of them will share their support of ethical companies via social media."* The source 29% is a *positive* statistic about ethical-brand sharing, not a *negative* statistic about complaints. Two options for resolution:

1. Revise the chapter sentence to use the positive framing as in source (e.g., "29% share their support of ethical companies via social media — which means the upside propagates as well").
2. Replace the 29% statistic with a different source-grounded statistic about negative-experience propagation (CLAUDE.md hard rule #1 requires source attribution for statistics).

Flag for Bear's review before any chapter that cites this stat is finalized. The rest of the ethics section's numbers (56% Mintel, $4.3M 5-Hour Energy, Lanham Act framing) trace cleanly to source.
